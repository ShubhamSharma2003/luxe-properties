About the Corridor

The Address That Defined Indian Luxury.

Golf Course Road runs eight kilometres from NH-48 in the north to the start of Golf Course Extension Road in the south, threading through the entirety of DLF Phase 5 — sectors 42, 43, 53, 54 and 55. For two decades, this single stretch has set the upper benchmark for Indian residential real estate.

The corridor is defined by three things. First: the DLF Golf & Country Club. Second: a near-complete monopoly by DLF, which has built and continues to operate the corridor's most iconic addresses — Camellias, Magnolias, Aralias, Crest. Third: Rapid Metro, which runs along the spine of the road with stations at every kilometre.

Where Golf Course Extension Road is the corridor of the next decade, Golf Course Road is the corridor of the last two — and remains, by some margin, the most expensive residential address in India.

Investment Thesis

Why Golf Course Road Holds Its Ceiling.

  • Supply scarcityNo major new launches on Golf Course Road itself in years. The DLF inventory is largely complete and the corridor is built out — pricing power flows directly from this scarcity.
  • Rapid Metro spineThe Rapid Metro line is unique to this corridor — no other Indian luxury market has integrated mass transit. This sustains rental yield and end-user demand.
  • DLF Golf Club anchorThe 18-hole DLF Golf & Country Club is a permanent infrastructure asset that no future masterplan can replicate. Direct golf views command 30–50% premium.
  • Resale liquidityHighest secondary-market liquidity of any Indian luxury corridor. Average days-on-market for ₹15-30 Cr apartments is 60–90 days, versus 180+ for comparable assets in other markets.
  • Corporate-executive demandCyber City proximity creates structural rental demand. Furnished 4 BHK rentals on the corridor command ₹4–8L per month with 4–6 week absorption.
  • Brand consistencyDLF's near-monopoly on the corridor has created a single-developer quality standard rare in luxury markets globally.
Frequently Asked Questions

Golf Course Road — Answered.

Golf Course Road is Gurgaon's flagship luxury real estate spine, running through DLF Phase 5 across sectors 42, 43, 53, 54 and 55. It is home to the DLF Golf & Country Club and the city's most iconic luxury addresses including DLF Camellias, Magnolias, Aralias and Crest.
Golf Course Road runs through sectors 42, 43, 53, 54 and 55 — the entirety of DLF Phase 5. Sector 42 hosts DLF Magnolias and Aralias. Sector 54 hosts DLF Camellias and the DLF Golf Club. Sector 55 marks the transition to Golf Course Extension Road.
As of 2026, prices on Golf Course Road range from ₹35,000 psf for established projects to ₹80,000+ psf at DLF Camellias. Total ticket sizes range from ₹8 Crore for older apartments to ₹100+ Crore for Camellias penthouses.
Golf Course Road (DLF Phase 5, sectors 42–55) is the established, ready-to-move luxury spine — dominated by DLF and home to ultra-luxury addresses like Camellias and Magnolias. Golf Course Extension Road (sectors 58–67) is newer, faster-appreciating, with more pre-launch and under-construction marquee projects from Oberoi, M3M, Trump and others.
Golf Course Road is exceptionally well-connected. Cyber City is 3–5 minutes, NH-48 is 5 minutes, IGI Airport is 18–20 minutes, and the Rapid Metro runs along the entire length of the road with multiple stations.
Golf Course Road has been one of India's most consistently appreciating luxury markets over the past 15 years. Strong end-user demand, scarce supply, and the prestige of DLF Phase 5 sustain pricing power. Resale liquidity is the highest of any Gurgaon luxury corridor.
DLF Camellias is the most expensive residential project on Golf Course Road and one of the most expensive in India. Apartments trade at ₹70,000–₹100,000+ per square foot, with penthouses reaching ₹150 Crore.
Major new launches on Golf Course Road itself are rare due to land scarcity. Recent launches include Godrej Golf Course Road (Sector 54) and Grand Hyatt Residences (Sector 55). For new launch volume, buyers typically look at the adjacent Golf Course Extension Road corridor.
